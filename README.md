##  Project Overview
This repository presents my **Digital Literacy Portfolio**, developed as part of the Digital Literacy course.

The objective of this project is to demonstrate awareness of responsible digital behavior, understanding of online safety and cybersecurity, ability to use digital tools professionally, and skills in effective online communication.

The portfolio is divided into five structured tasks, each focusing on a specific aspect of digital literacy.

##  Task Details

###  Task 1: Digital Literacy Infographic
A visually engaging infographic created using Canva to explain the concept of digital literacy.

It highlights safe and responsible internet usage, essential digital tools for students, and ways to maintain a professional online identity.

Directory: task-1-presentation/

---

###  Task 2: Digital Portfolio
This task focuses on building a professional online presence by creating profiles on GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle.

These platforms help in showcasing projects, networking professionally, and exploring data science and machine learning resources. Screenshots of all profiles are included.

Directory: task-2-portfolio/

---

###  Task 3: Coding & Collaboration Platforms
In this task, I completed a beginner-level coding challenge on HackerRank to gain hands-on programming experience.

Additionally, I created a Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz using Google Forms to assess students’ knowledge of safe and responsible digital practices.

---

###  Task 4: Email Etiquette & Social Media Responsibility
This task demonstrates proper digital communication through two professional email drafts: one requesting an assignment extension and another expressing interest in a summer internship.

It also includes a checklist outlining responsible social media practices for students.

Directory: task-4-email-etiquette/

---

###  Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness
This task includes a detailed case study on cybercrime, focusing on types of online threats, their impact, and preventive measures.

A practical safety checklist is also provided to help students stay secure while using digital platforms.

Directory: task-5-cybercrime/
